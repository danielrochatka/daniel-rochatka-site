This manuscript is a follow-up to the published study on placental thickness and severity-weighted fetal dysfunction ([Journal of Clinical Medicine, 2025](/research/placental-thickness-jcm/)). Where the prior paper established correlations between placental thickness and fetal cardiac outcomes, this study focuses on the maternal side of that relationship.

The design is a matched case-control study comparing 99 pregnancies with marked third-trimester placental thickening (≥70 mm) against 99 gestational-age-matched controls. The primary focus is maternal infectious, hormonal, metabolic, and endocrine burden, alongside associated fetal findings, to characterize what maternal factors accompany the placental thickening pattern identified in the prior study.
