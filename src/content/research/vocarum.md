The Vocarum research paper introduces the framework for ontology-framed, multi-model consensus journalism and describes the key architectural decisions that distinguish it from both traditional journalism tools and generic AI summarization.

The core claim of the paper is that meaningful consensus requires not only agreement but a structured account of where agreement exists, where it does not, and what kinds of disagreement are present. Factual disagreement, methodological difference, definitional conflict, and temporal scope differences are not the same thing, and a useful information system should make these distinctions explicit rather than averaging them away.

The paper covers source aggregation, claim extraction, multi-model interpretation, consensus mapping, divergence classification, and versioned publication with provenance tracking.
