The Vocarum paper introduces the conceptual and architectural basis for treating model disagreement as a first-class input rather than noise to be suppressed. Where most AI journalism tools aim to produce a single authoritative summary, Vocarum treats convergence and divergence as information: the output of the system is not "what happened" but a structured account of where multiple independent models agree, where they diverge, and what kind of divergence each case represents.

Factual disagreement, methodological difference, definitional conflict, and temporal-scope difference are not the same thing. A system that averages across them loses the signal. Vocarum's architecture makes these distinctions explicit through ontology-framed consensus mapping, source aggregation, claim extraction, multi-model interpretation, and divergence classification.

The paper documents the model intersection pipeline, the manifest-based provenance system, and the architectural decisions that distinguish Vocarum from both traditional journalism tools and generic AI summarization.

A provisional patent application was filed with the USPTO in 2025. The [Vocarum project page](/projects/vocarum/) covers the broader system design.
