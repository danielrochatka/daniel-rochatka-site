Standard cosmological models treat cosmological redshift and the galactic rotation anomaly as separate phenomena requiring separate explanatory additions — dark energy for the expansion-driven redshift, and dark matter for the anomalous flatness of galactic rotation curves. This paper examines whether a unified gravitational framework under a finite-universe assumption can account for both observations without invoking either addition.

The central argument is that the finite extent of the universe changes the boundary conditions on gravitational fields in ways that produce measurable consequences at cosmological and galactic scales. Redshift is reframed as a gravitational consequence of the finite-universe geometry rather than exclusively a Doppler signature of expansion. The rotation anomaly is examined under the same framework: whether the modified gravitational field in a finite volume, without separate dark-matter halos, can reproduce the observed flatness of rotation curves.

The paper documents the theoretical framework, the derivation of observable predictions, and the comparison against astronomical data. It is related to the broader Transformational Physics Framework program documented in [Gravitational Energy Reframed](/research/transformational-physics-framework/).

Companion simulation work was conducted in the [Galactic Dynamics Lab](https://github.com/danielrochatka/galactic-dynamics-lab), a C++ galaxy and orbital simulation engine with configurable physics packages including Newtonian and TPFCore modes.
