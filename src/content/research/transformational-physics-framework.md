The Transformational Physics Framework is a foundational research program aimed at reformulating the structure of classical mechanics. The specific question: if you begin with transformations and their conservation properties as primary, can you recover the standard equations of mechanics as consequences rather than starting points?

This is a question about the logical architecture of the theory, not about whether the established equations are correct. The established equations are not in question. The question is whether there is a different formal starting point from which they emerge, and whether that starting point makes some aspects of the theory's structure more transparent.

Newtonian and Lagrangian mechanics begin from energy or force as primitive concepts, with conservation laws derived as theorems. TPF inverts this: transformations and their invariances are the primitives, and energy is an accounting structure that emerges from them.

Controlled simulation verification through the Galactic Dynamics Lab confirmed that the implemented TPF weak-field correspondence route reproduces Newtonian acceleration and trajectory behavior within recorded numerical precision. That correspondence is a necessary property of a viable framework, not the research claim.

A manuscript documenting the full theoretical framework is in preparation. A DOI will be added when assigned.
