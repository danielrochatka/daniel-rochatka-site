The Transformational Physics Framework addresses a foundational structural question in classical mechanics: if you begin with transformations and their conservation properties as the primitive concepts rather than energy, can you recover the standard equations of mechanics as consequences? The established equations are not in question. The question is whether there is a different formal starting point from which they emerge, and whether that starting point makes aspects of the theory's structure more transparent.

Conventional mechanics begins from energy or force as primitive concepts, with conservation laws derived as theorems. TPF inverts this: transformations and their invariances are the primitives, and energy is an accounting structure that emerges from them. The paper documents the gravitational energy case under this reframing — how gravitational potential energy is re-derived and what the reformulation clarifies about the relationship between gravitational and kinetic energy in bound systems.

Controlled simulation verification through the [Galactic Dynamics Lab](https://github.com/danielrochatka/galactic-dynamics-lab) confirmed that the implemented TPF weak-field correspondence route reproduces Newtonian acceleration and trajectory behavior within recorded numerical precision across single-source, galaxy-style, and small-N scenarios. That correspondence check is a necessary property for a viable framework, not the novel result.
