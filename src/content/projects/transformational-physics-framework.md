The Transformational Physics Framework is an independent research program aimed at a specific foundational question: what happens if you formulate dynamics from the structure of transformations and their conservation properties, rather than beginning from energy as the primitive concept?

The conventional approach in classical mechanics starts from energy, uses it to derive equations of motion, and treats conservation as a consequence. TPF inverts this priority. The goal is to recover the established equations of mechanics as a consequence of a different starting point — one that makes the role of transformations and their invariances explicit from the beginning — and to explore what, if anything, this reframing makes visible that the conventional formulation obscures.

Controlled simulation tests conducted through the Galactic Dynamics Lab (see the [public repository](https://github.com/danielrochatka/galactic-dynamics-lab)) verified that the implemented TPF weak-field correspondence route reproduces Newtonian acceleration and trajectory behavior within recorded numerical precision across single-source, galaxy-style, and small-N scenarios. That correspondence check is a necessary property for a viable framework, not a novel result.
