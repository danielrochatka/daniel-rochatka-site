Atom is the primary applied AI system I designed and built. The central design problem is one that conventional retrieval and generic AI do not solve well: how do you make a specialized body of documents usable as a governed evidence workspace, where conclusions remain traceable to their source passages and can be reviewed, updated, and reused over time?

The platform combines planning, multi-stage retrieval, verification, claim extraction, concordance mapping, and operational traceability. Answers remain attached to supporting passages and source documents rather than floating free as detached summaries. Coverage signals expose where analysis is complete, partial, or stale.

My architecture work spans the retrieval and routing system, the evidence schema, the claim intelligence structures, concordance analysis, and the governed workspace model that allows institutional corpora to persist as searchable, reviewable knowledge assets rather than ephemeral context windows.

Atom is commercially available. Further detail about its capabilities is at [atomworkspace.com](https://atomworkspace.com) and on its [Procyonsoft project page](https://procyonsoft.com/projects/atom/).
