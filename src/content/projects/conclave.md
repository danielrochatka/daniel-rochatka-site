A Procyonsoft project conceived and architected by Daniel Rochatka.

Conclave is a structured deliberation platform for human and AI teams. Where ordinary multi-agent conversation allows discussion to continue indefinitely without clear authority or closure, Conclave organizes every session around an explicit goal, defined participant roles, and a governed path from deliberation to durable resolution.

The design addresses a failure mode specific to collaborative AI systems: useful deliberation does not require consensus, but it does require stopping conditions. A Conclave session may close with a decision, preserved dissent, a deferral, or an escalation to human judgment — each recorded explicitly alongside the supporting rationale, evidence, and unresolved concerns.

Participants operate within role boundaries. Human authority is a structural feature: people can introduce new information, change the goal, approve or reject a proposed resolution, and reopen completed sessions. The platform governs when a discussion is complete; humans govern what the decision means and what happens next.

The project is in incubation as a product direction on the Procyonsoft platform. Further detail is on the [Procyonsoft project page](https://procyonsoft.com/projects/conclave/).
